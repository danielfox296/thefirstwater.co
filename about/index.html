About

You feel it
before you can explain it.

I make music for a living, and somewhere in all those years of sessions I learned the thing every producer learns eventually: people feel a record before they have a single thought about it. I have spent my working life trying to make that first moment happen on purpose, in songs, and now in rooms.

The practice

Rooms built the way records are built

Sound Sessions is that work at room scale. Each night is a facilitated group experience built on engineered sound: a room in low light, sub-bass you feel in your sternum, a formal opening and a formal close, and seventy-five minutes with nothing to do and nothing to say.

Every session is built around something worth putting down. The first nights are for people carrying the end of a relationship, and more are coming behind them, each with its own reason to lie down on the floor.

The craft

The bet is production

Nearly everything I believe about this practice comes down to how the room sounds. A room can sound like a gymnasium or it can sound like the inside of a cello, and the difference between the two is the same discipline I use to make records: sub-bass placed for the chest, arcs built the way songs are built, a space treated like a studio instead of a stage. That is what I know how to do, so that is the bet.
